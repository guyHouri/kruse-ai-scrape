Lack of triplet oxygen affects Type 1 collagen, explaining skull deformations without head-binding Triplet oxygen is ground-state molecular oxygen with two unpaired electrons in parallel spin. Type 1 collagen synthesis requires oxygen for hydroxylation of proline and lysine residues by prolyl hydroxylase and lysyl hydroxylase enzymes.
